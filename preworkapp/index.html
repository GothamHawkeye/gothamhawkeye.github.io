Welcome to My Webpage

I'm Scott Isebrand. I was born and raised in Iowa, lived briefly in Connecticut, and moved to New York City in 1997 to work in marketing in educational publishing. From 2001, I worked for a special events facility in Manhattan, initially in marketing, then in administration.

My Path to Boot Camp

The Pandemic & a Civil War General

During the first months of the COVID pandemic, I watched the documentary Grant, about Ulysses S. Grant, the victorious commanding-general of the U.S. Army during the American Civil War and twice-elected 18th president of the United States. Afterwards I thought that he should be better known. At the same time, I had become interested in social media and digital marketing, and wondered about changing to a tech-focused career. I decided that a web project about U.S. Grant would be interesting for me, and well timed because 2022 would be the bicentennial of Grant's birth.

WordPress Project

I knew nothing about coding. So I used to the open-source website-builder WordPress and a customizeable WordPress theme called TheGem to launch my site Ulysses S. Grant Revealed, (GrantRevealed.com).

I enjoyed creating content for the website, embedding images and video in blog posts, and playing around with formatting through the WordPress. But I often had to open a ticket with the TheGem's tech support team when parts of the website didn't work as I expected. Their solution would almost always be to adjust or add CSS code. From those experiences, I began to appreciate the power of understanding Web development.

Figuring Out the Magic

For most people, the ubiquitous digital reality we all intereact with daily is "magic on a screen," as a friend said, and the code behind it like unimaginably inscrutable spells. But knowledge is power, and I hope to learn coding at Per Scholas—figuring out mysteries of both frontend and backend development—to forge a new career as a software developer...a modern-day magician of sorts!

Grant

It inspired me to create a website about him.
